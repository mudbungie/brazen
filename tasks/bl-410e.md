Follow-up to bl-98d1 (reasoning items now open a Thinking block on the reasoning item add) and bl-aba5 (fixtures are hand-authored, not live-captured).

TWO things to settle, both needing a REAL Responses reasoning capture (o-series / gpt-5 reasoning, tiny request, recorded SSE):

1. VERIFY THE WIRE SHAPE. bl-98d1 assumes:
   - response.output_item.added carries item.type=="reasoning"
   - response.reasoning_summary_text.delta carries (output_index, summary_index) and NO content_index
   so the deltas route to pair (output_index, 0) — the block opened on the item add.
   If a real capture shows a content_index on those deltas, the routing pair differs and the fix in src/protocol/openai_responses/decode/mod.rs (item_added reasoning arm) needs revisiting. The test in tests/responses_decode_errors.rs (a_reasoning_item_opens_a_thinking_block_its_summary_delta_routes_in) is hand-authored from the same assumption.

2. DECIDE THE CANONICAL REPRESENTATION (one block vs per-part). Today all summary parts (summary_index 0,1,..) COLLAPSE into one canonical Thinking block at pair (output_index,0) and concatenate with no separator — matches Anthropic's single-thinking-block model; ContentKind::Thinking {} has no part slot. This is deliberate and can't drop data. If a consumer ever needs per-part reasoning, the change is small and SYMMETRIC with the text path: open per-part on response.reasoning_summary_part.added (like content_part.added for text) and have part_key() read summary_index for reasoning events (like content_index for text). The seam is part_key() (one place picks which wire field is the sub-index) + where the block opens. Don't pre-build it — it's a representation decision that needs a real consumer need + a capture showing how many summary parts the models actually emit. Spec providers.md §3.4 documents the collapse.

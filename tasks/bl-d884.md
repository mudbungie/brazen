Spun out of bl-7e50 (raw reasoning_text.delta now routes to Thinking) + bl-410e/CR-R4.

PROBLEM. Two reasoning channels on a Responses 'reasoning' output item both route to the SAME canonical pair (output_index, 0) and append to the one Thinking block's buffer in wire-ARRIVAL order:
  - summary channel: response.reasoning_summary_text.delta (the item's summary[] array)
  - raw channel:     response.reasoning_text.delta        (the item's content[] array)
If a single response ever emits BOTH (e.g. caller sets reasoning.summary AND opts into raw content), their deltas INTERLEAVE into one buffer → the Thinking text can come out garbled/incoherent (summary fragment, raw fragment, summary fragment, ...), not merely redundant. Lossless in bytes, but not readable.

UNKNOWN (blocks the fix, needs a real capture). Do the two channels actually co-occur in one request, or are they mutually exclusive by config? A real gpt-5/o-series SSE capture settles this (and also resolves the CR-R4 summary-part-count question in the same shot). No OpenAI key in env today — capture needs a key + a paid call.

LIKELY FIX (decide after capture). A coexistence rule at the dispatch/routing seam:
  - prefer ONE channel (raw-wins or summary-wins) and drop the other, OR
  - route them to DISTINCT Thinking blocks — but canonical ContentKind::Thinking {} has no part slot, so this is the per-part reopening already scoped in CR-R4 (open per-part, part_key() reads the sub-index). 
The seam is the same one CR-R4 names: part_key() + where the block opens, in src/protocol/openai_responses/decode/mod.rs.

DELIVERABLE: capture -> confirm/deny coexistence -> if real, a coexistence rule + a test pinning interleave behavior; if they never co-occur, downgrade to a documented impossibility in CR-R4. Update spec §3.4/CR-R4 either way.

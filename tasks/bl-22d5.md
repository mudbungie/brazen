## The misdiagnosis
bl-cc84 concluded the codex backend "DROPPED its stream:true mandate" because a `stream:false` request returned 200 where bl-b72f had recorded a 400. That conclusion is almost certainly WRONG. The 200 is fully explained by a change in BRAZEN, not codex.

## What actually changed (timeline, all 2026-06-16)
- 23:34 `dd33e18` [bl-20d5] "Stream by default".
- 23:51 `b24e268` [bl-b72f] fuzz harness records `stream:false -> 400 "Stream must be set to true"` — REAL, on-wire (forcing did not yet exist).
- 23:57 `a9ccece` [bl-9e3d] "Decide: support explicit stream:false" adds the HARD force `req.stream = Some(true)` at `src/run/serve.rs:112`, on the canonical (non-`--raw`) path, AFTER fill/strip. Lands 6 min AFTER bl-b72f.

`git merge-base --is-ancestor a9ccece b24e268` -> NO: the forcing came after bl-b72f.

## Why the 200 proves nothing about codex
`src/run/serve.rs:93-112` (canonical path): the stdin JSON's `"stream":false` parses into the TYPED `req.stream` field, then `req.stream = Some(true)` overwrites it unconditionally before `encode`. The literal comment: "streaming is FORCED, overriding any request/config `stream:false` ... The typed `stream` field exists to intercept the key here so no `false` slips through the `extra` long-tail valve to the wire; exact non-stream wire control is `--raw`'s territory (config 4.2)."

So codex RECEIVES `stream:true` and returns 200 — exactly as forcing dictates. The fuzz harness (`bz/tests/live_support/exec.rs::run_bz`, argv `--provider/--model/--json`, NO `--raw`) and bl-cc84's manual `bz ... --json` evidence BOTH use this path. Neither can put `stream:false` on the wire. Codex's CURRENT stream-mandate status is therefore UNVERIFIED by either run.

The asymmetry is the tell: `store`/`system` are passed through to the wire (untyped `store` rides `extra`; `system` -> instructions), so their mandates are still genuinely testable and "still hold". `stream` is typed-and-forced, so it is structurally unreachable — which is why it alone looked "dropped".
